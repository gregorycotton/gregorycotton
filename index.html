Currently improving end-user experiences at Nokia. Formerly a product designer with the Ontario Digital Service.

 

I believe that good design exists in whatever medium is best suited to prioritize inclusive, meaningful, and impactful solutions. It isn’t inherently digital-first, nor is it solely about aesthetics and good font choice – as fun and important as that may be. Good design is about addressing inefficiencies and inequalities, about leveraging existing systems or building new ones to improve quality of life, foster togetherness, and to encourage a sustainable long-term mindset in regards to our world, our systems, and our products/services.

 

I got my start in experience design studying Global Business and Digital Arts at the University of Waterloo’s Stratford School of Interaction Design and Business. My time at the University of Waterloo offered me the opportunity to study in Hong Kong, at City University’s School of Creative Media, where I took classes in human-computer interaction and art history.
