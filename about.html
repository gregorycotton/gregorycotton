Hi, I'm Greg, a visual artist and product designer from Canada.

Click here to skip to all the important links, or read on if you're dying to know just who I am.

 

 

I was born in Ottawa, Ontario, where I spent most of my life until up and moving to Waterloo (and later Stratford) to study Global Business and Digital Arts at the University of Waterloo's Stratford School of Interaction Design and Business (class of 2021). I was also fortunate enough to spend my 3A semester abroad, studying at the City University of Hong Kong.

 

 

As a kid I used to read road maps and took a special interest in the chapter of the driver's book with the street sign pictures. I'd like to think it was because I was interested in simple visual communication, but more likely than that, I just had weird hobbies. Regardless, the former is true of me today. From the boxy hockey player drawings I did in Kindergarten to the work I'm proud to put forward in my portfolio today, I've been interested in 'design' for a while — which I guess is pretty vague.

 

 

For me, design isn't just good-lookin' UI and graphics, as enjoyable and important as those things are. Design is a means of problem solving; an inclusive process that identifies pain points, inefficiencies, and inequalities in our systems and products, how they exist and what facilitates them. Design works towards making things better for everyone involved. Design isn't inherently visual. Designing solutions should not be a solo activity. One of my favourite quotes is from IDEO's Tim Brown: "Design has become too important to be left to designers." Whether we want to build better products, work to reimagine our future, or improve existing services, we will always achieve the best results by working together.

 

 

That all being said, I do take great pleasure in using my free time to create art. I've been interested in photography for the better half of a decade now and have been drawing for much longer. Over the years, as I've been introduced to new mediums, styles, and art in general, both the style of my work and the mediums I choose to use have shifted. I've had a blast experimenting with generative art among many other things and exploring topics such as food sustainability and geography (also among many other things).

 

 

If your thirst to get to know me has still yet to be quenched, feel free to reach out to me to chat about whatever. If you think this was all excessive, too much info, that you don't need to know all this about me, then I'd have to ask what prompted you to read all the way to the end, and what's keeping you reading now.
