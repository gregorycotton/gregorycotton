Language is a luxury
September 4, 2023

–

In August 2023 I moved to Lisbon for work, for an indefinite period of time. Specifically, I moved to a village about a 40 minute train ride from downtown Lisbon. In the months leading up to the move I did a bit of learning the Portuguese language through Duolingo, but frankly, not nearly enough to be considered conversational.

When I walk down the street the ambience of others around me is never familiar. I hardly understand a word of it. When I look at a poster I can only decipher any of what it seeks to communicate through visual cues.

When defining luxury, “the state of great comfort and extravagant living”, I think our minds often wander to material goods. Big houses, expensive food, designer clothing. But over the last seven days since I moved to the outskirts of Lisbon, it’s certainly been made clear to me how truly one of the greatest luxuries is to live in a community where communication amongst community members is seamless. The ability to understand and interpret your surroundings, to share a story or ask a question to another without giving the means by which you communicate a second though, that is truly to be in a state of great comfort.

This isn’t that novel of an observation – I have no doubt anyone who’s immigrated to a new country has experience some shade of what I’m experiencing. This also isn’t to say I don’t like it here because of this barrier — I so far quite enjoy Lisbon, as well as the village I’m staying in, and am eager to learn to speak Portuguese properly so that I can communicate with others. But it’s no doubt that this recent change has made me more appreciative for a luxury that I took for granted for a majority of my life.
