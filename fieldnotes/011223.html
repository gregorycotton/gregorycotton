On not knowing things / believing in magic
WIP

–

In my late teenage years, when questions of any sort worked their ways into convos, it was pretty normal for my brother or myself to just look up the answer online. This led to my mother adopting the phrase “Why wonder when you can Google?”, and using it quite frequently.

In more recent years however, I’ve found I often prefer to wonder. Maybe not about important happenings around the world, but about the menial questions that jump into my head or that I encounter in passing, day to day. ‘Ignorance is bliss’, is how the saying goes.

With the how fast the accessibility of information through technology is growing and the societal pressure that comes with a culture that values expertise and defines intelligence by way of knowledge accumulation, my experience is it can be easy to feel as if you need to not only know, but have an opinion on everything. Even when I’m on my own, I feel pressure to know something; an itch to instantly address any question I have that is so easy to scratch.

A few months ago, I was giving thought to the patterns on my cat. I am entirely unsure of how pattern in the fur of cats emerges – how different follicles adapt different colours in a way that reminds me a bit of a perlin noise map, or something of the sort. There is no doubt a very digestible explanation as of to why that is a few keystrokes away… but I personally would rather remain ignorant to this information and instead maintain the belief that cats just have little bit of magic in them.

Sometimes its best to maintain childlike ignorance and wonder and believe the world is made up of magic.
