Personal Projects.


When it comes to my personal work I love to experiment, both visually and thematically. My projects generally take a bit of time to properly conceptualize and create, and they often blend elements of photography, graphic design, mixed media art and even entry-level computer science, to name a few.

    Misc. Etc.


    I am fascinated by large, high-density cities. One of the most staggering elements of Hong Kong to me is the scale of everything; from the height of the buildings, to the number of people. I thought that as a response to this surreal density it would be interesting to create a series of pieces that were equally busy themselves by combining shots of buildings in an HDR-like manner.

     

     

     

     

    Each image in this set is composed of 50 images (a grand total of 200 unique photos taken). The first leg of this project was done out and about around the city, taking the photos. I subsequently edited the photos in Lightroom, and then brought them into Photoshop. In Photoshop I merged images as HDR files in groups of ten, then would import the five HDR files of ten images each I had created into Photoshop, where I manually composited them from there using layer masks, blending modes and various other techniques.
